Read The Body Paragraphs of This Letter

I am sending a frank to Mrs. Hofland & I cannot help enclosing a note to you to ask if the report in the papers be true that Lazarus is sold? I hope it is so--I hope so so earnestly that I can hardly believe it.

Did I tell you that I had had an application from Mr. Elliston for Foscari? which under Mr. Macready's advice I declined. But the application was flattering--& I trust that under all the disadvantage of the execrable acting a great impression has been made by Julian--to have withstood such acting for four nights is something--Is it not?--& if we go on in the Easter week I shall have hopes--& if we do not I shall not despond--for much has been done of that I am confident. The article in the John Bull was what I expected--but that in The Examiner was worse in my mind--Could not you do any thing to procure a short & more favorable notice against next Sunday? I ask you just as I would ask a brother--I wish with all my heart that any one would write an exposure of that slang of criticisim "Melodramatic"--I am sure, as Mr. Macready says, that if any one of the Greek Dramas or even Shakespeare's stronger plays, or any thing that is effective were to be produced now, it would be afraid by that word.--By the way Mr. Macready and his Sister have been kind to me beyond all kindness. They are delightful people.

Will you say every thing for me to your lovely wife--& my kind friends Mr. Bewick & Mr. Chatfield? Shall I send you some orders for the Fifth night?Mitford is probably refering to the fifth night of production of her play, Julian, at Covent Garden. An article in the Morning Post lists the fifth night of production as 02 April 1823.--And pray try what you can do with The Examiner.--Ever my dear Sir

Kind regards from Papa & Mama.
